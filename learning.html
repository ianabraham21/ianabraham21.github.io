Active Learning

This part of my work entails looking towards biology for inspiration towards the development of algorithms that exploit sensor dynamics for active sensings. In particular, rat vibrissae is studied as a focal point towards understanding how the sense touch can be used more effectively in robotic systems.
Currently, I am implementing active sensing and exploration strategies that utilize the structure of the whisker arrray for shape estimation and localization of objects.

About Me

I recieved by B.S. degree in Mechanical Engineering from Rutgers University, New Brunswick, NJ in 2014. I received my M.S. degree in Mechanical Engineering from Northwestern University and I am currently a PhD candidate in the Neuroscience and Robotics Lab (NxR) at Northwestern University, Evanston, IL. My current research interests are real-time algorithms for active sensing, learning, and data-driven control in robotic systems.
