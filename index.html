Research

My research involves enabling robotic systems to actively sense and learn about their environment subject to their physical constraints. Specifically, my goal is to develop real-time algorithms that enable robots to autonomously adapt and learn in any environment.

Object Shape Estimation

Active Learning

Tactile-based Object Localization

Bioinspired Sensing

About Me

I recieved by B.S. degree in Mechanical Engineering from Rutgers University, New Brunswick, NJ in 2014. I received my M.S. degree in Mechanical Engineering from Northwestern University and I am currently a PhD candidate in the Neuroscience and Robotics Lab (NxR) at Northwestern University, Evanston, IL. My current research interests are real-time algorithms for active sensing, learning, and data-driven control in robotic systems.
